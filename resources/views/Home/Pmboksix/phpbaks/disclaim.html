PMWay disclaimer

In line with the spirit of openness that is the world wide web, please take note that, in addition to creating the PMWay (my take on projectization), I have also consolidated information from numerous sources into the melting pot that is this web.

As such, it is not my intention to misrepresent or claim ownership for work that is not my own.

Rather my goal is simply to pull together information towards a pooling of knowledge, primarily as a more easy to access consolidation for myself (and for project resources working on my projects, managers, executives etc.), but also for interested visitors who may find the PMWay mindset (spin on the subject / distillation of the essence of Project Management, CMMi etc.) more informative, easy to understand and accessible.  To help this along I have also made liberal use of pictures and cartoons etc. and at times may also have used a lighter "fun" treatment (take) on material that on official courses would probably be dealt with more formally. 

While the official material and courses (and certification) are always highly recommended, my hope is that PMWay will act as an "entr√©e" and door opener and a stimulus for deeper thought and debate on the issues (Key Concepts) raised, helping to improve understanding and to motivate readers to further their knowledge etc.  Also I hope that PMWay will entice many to embrace project management process and principals, who would probably, due to the formal (and esoteric) nature of the official courses, have felt or been excluded.  The Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK®) contains very precise and clearly defined project management processes which should always be followed as has been set down.  A summary dashboard of this can be seen if you click here.  So also does Prince2 and other methodologies each have their own defined processes.  Understanding that at an essential level, a project is a project is a project, then what is found in PMWay (via the Sitemap) is simply a 'fun fleshing out' of concepts to facilitate deeper understanding towards a more enlightened engagement (as a first prize) of the methodology you decide to follow and an upping of production stats the result.

Remember projects were being run and managed successfully thousands or years ago (building the pyramids in Egypt etc.) and have been done so by enthusiastic people for thousands of years with no qualifications!  I.e. people have found ways to work together participating in and contribute towards goals / for the greater good!  However, in a technologically advanced and computerized world, and as we no longer live in fear, as they did under the omnipotent power and whip of the goal and resource focusing Pharaoh in ancient times, and even more so as South Africa which like France, Britain etc. is highly Unionized, we have to find another way to focus and channel resources towards the achievement of goals.

To this end an understanding of the next slide from the CMMi manual is absolutely key to success!

Hopefully PMWay will begin to expose the essence and form of the above to you.

Finally, while the official material and the defined processes (knowledge areas etc.) that underpin these should always be referred to first and relied upon, please realize that I do fully understand that opinions, as they say, are like noses on faces!
I.e. everyone has a slightly different and in their opinion better opinion!
As such I (within PMWay) do not profess to have all the answers! 
I am sure there are other better opinions out there (as we aspire to pin down "true form" of the areas raised). 
Also I work on this web part time and when I can find spare time.  Therefore (per Linus's Law:  "many eyeballs make bugs shallow"), I am always open to receive criticism and other opinions if you want to engage with me in constructive manner.  I.e. any and all comment and feedback that improves PMWay is welcomed.  And if you find errors or feel you can make recommendations you are asked to "snip" them to me or you can email me at mark@pmway.co.za.
